# Negotiation Playbook Skill

## Purpose

Provide tailored negotiation strategies, scripts, and tactics for job offers. Help users maximize their compensation while maintaining positive relationships with prospective employers.

## Trigger Phrases

- "Help me negotiate with [company]"
- "I got an offer from [company]"
- "How do I counter this offer?"
- "Negotiation script for [company]"
- "They asked about my salary expectations"
- "How do I handle the compensation question?"

## Required Inputs

1. **Company Name** (required)
2. **Offer Details** (if received)
   - Base salary
   - Equity/stock
   - Bonus
   - Benefits
   - Start date
3. **User's Situation**
   - Other offers?
   - Current compensation?
   - Must-haves vs nice-to-haves?
4. **Salary Research** (from Salary Recon skill or user-provided)

## Negotiation Scenarios

### Scenario A: Salary Expectation Question (Pre-Offer)

**When asked**: "What are your salary expectations?"

**Strategy**: Defer without appearing evasive

**Scripts**:

**Option 1 - Redirect to Research**:
> "I'm focused on finding the right fit and I'm confident we can work out compensation if we're aligned on the role. I'd love to understand the range you've budgeted for this position so I can confirm we're in the same ballpark."

**Option 2 - Range Based on Research** (if pressed):
> "Based on my research for similar roles at this level, I'm seeing total compensation in the $X to $Y range. I'm flexible within that range depending on the full package structure and opportunity."

**Option 3 - Current Comp Deflection** (if asked current salary):
> "I'd prefer to focus on the value I'll bring to this role rather than my current compensation. [In many states, this is also legally protected.] What range did you have in mind for this position?"

**Red Lines**:
- Never give a single number (always a range)
- Never low-ball yourself to seem "reasonable"
- Don't lie about current compensation

---

### Scenario B: Initial Offer Received

**Framework: PAUSE → ANALYZE → STRATEGIZE → RESPOND**

#### Step 1: PAUSE
Always ask for time to review:

> "Thank you so much for the offer—I'm really excited about the opportunity to join [Company]. I'd like to take a day or two to review the details carefully. Could you send the offer in writing so I can review the complete package?"

**Never negotiate in the moment of receiving an offer.**

#### Step 2: ANALYZE

Create offer breakdown:

| Component | Offered | Market Rate | Gap | Priority |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-----|----------|
| Base | $X | $Y | -$Z | High |
| Equity | $A | $B | -$C | High |
| Sign-on | $D | $E | -$F | Medium |
| Bonus % | G% | H% | -I% | Medium |
| Benefits | [list] | [compare] | - | Low |

**Questions to assess**:
1. Is base at or above your minimum?
2. How does equity compare to market?
3. What's the total comp vs your target?
4. What non-monetary factors matter?

#### Step 3: STRATEGIZE

Determine approach based on gap:

| Gap Size | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| < 10% below target | Light negotiation, focus on non-cash |
| 10-20% below target | Standard counter, multiple levers |
| > 20% below target | Significant counter, be prepared to walk |

**Identify leverage**:
- Other offers (strongest leverage)
- Unique skills/experience match
- Urgency of their need
- Market conditions

#### Step 4: RESPOND

See scripts below.

### Scenario C: Counter-Offer Scripts

**Script 1: Enthusiastic but Seeking Alignment**

> "Thank you for the offer—I'm genuinely excited about [Company] and the opportunity to [specific thing about role]. After reviewing the package and reflecting on both my experience and the market, I'd like to discuss a few adjustments to reach alignment.
>
> For base salary, I was hoping for $[X], which reflects [rationale: market data, my experience level, specific skills]. 
>
> For equity, given [company stage/my level], I'd like to explore increasing the grant to $[Y] over the vesting period.
>
> I'm confident we can find a package that works for both of us. Are you able to discuss these adjustments?"

**Script 2: Multiple Levers Approach**

> "I'm very excited about this opportunity and want to make this work. Looking at the complete package, I have a few areas I'd like to discuss:
>
> 1. **Base**: Would $[X] be possible? This aligns with [rationale].
> 2. **Equity**: Could we increase the grant to [Y shares / $Y value]?
> 3. **Sign-on**: Would a sign-on bonus of $[Z] be an option to bridge the gap?
>
> I want to be reasonable and understand there may be constraints on some components. What flexibility do you have across these areas?"

**Script 3: With Competing Offer**

> "Thank you for the offer. I want to be transparent—I'm also in final stages with [another company / another opportunity] that has offered [higher base / more equity / compelling package].
>
> [Company] remains my top choice because [genuine reason], and I'd love to make this work. Is there flexibility to move the base to $[X] and equity to [Y]? That would make this an easy decision for me."

**Script 4: Accepting with Minor Ask**

> "I'm thrilled to accept this offer and join [Company]. Before I sign, would you be able to [small ask: additional week PTO / modest sign-on / accelerated review]? This would really start things off on the right note."

### Scenario D: Responding to Pushback

**If they say "This is our final offer"**:

> "I understand, and I appreciate you being direct. Let me ask—if base is firm, is there flexibility on [sign-on / equity / start date bonus / title / PTO]?"

**If they need justification**:

> "Absolutely, I'm happy to explain my thinking. Based on [Levels.fyi data / market research / comparable offers], VP Engineering roles at [similar companies] are compensated in the $[X-Y] range. Given my experience with [specific relevant experience], I believe $[target] is well-aligned with the market and the value I'll bring."

**If they say "We pay everyone at this level the same"**:

> "I appreciate the commitment to equity. Could we discuss the level itself? Given my background in [specific experience], I wonder if [higher level] might be appropriate, which would also address the compensation alignment."

**If they go silent**:

Wait 2-3 business days, then:

> "I wanted to follow up on our compensation discussion. I remain very interested in [Company] and hope we can find a way to work together. Do you have any updates?"

---

### Scenario E: Handling Specific Components

**Equity Negotiation**:

For startups:
> "I'm excited about the equity component. To help me evaluate it, could you share the current valuation, total shares outstanding, and preferred share price from the last round? Also, what vesting acceleration provisions exist for change of control?"

For public companies:
> "Could we discuss increasing the RSU grant? Given [market data / my level], I was hoping for [X] shares over the vesting period. Is there flexibility there?"

**Sign-on Bonus**:

> "I understand base may be constrained by bands. Would a sign-on bonus of $[X] be possible to bridge the gap? This is common for candidates at this level making a transition."

**Start Date**:

> "Would it be possible to start on [later date]? I want to [wrap up current projects / take time between roles / personal reason]. In exchange, I'm happy to [begin advising / review materials / meet the team] before my official start."

**Title Negotiation**:

> "I noticed the title is [offered title]. Given my scope of responsibility and experience, would [preferred title] be possible? This is important for [future career trajectory / industry recognition / team credibility]."

## Negotiation Principles

### DO:

✅ **Express enthusiasm first, always**
- They need to believe you want the job
- Negotiation should feel collaborative, not adversarial

✅ **Use silence strategically**
- After making an ask, stop talking
- Let them respond without filling the gap

✅ **Negotiate multiple components simultaneously**
- "I'd like to discuss base, equity, and sign-on"
- Gives them flexibility to say yes somewhere

✅ **Have a BATNA (Best Alternative)**
- Know your walk-away point
- Other offers are the best leverage

✅ **Get everything in writing**
- Verbal agreements can be forgotten
- Ask for updated offer letter before accepting

✅ **Be patient**
- Good negotiation takes time
- Rushed decisions favor the employer

### DON'T:

❌ **Never lie**
- Don't fabricate other offers
- Don't misrepresent current comp
- Integrity matters and can backfire

❌ **Never threaten**
- "I'll leave if you don't..."
- Keep it collaborative

❌ **Never accept immediately**
- Even if you're thrilled
- You lose all leverage

❌ **Never negotiate against yourself**
- State your ask, then wait
- Don't lower your number without a counter

❌ **Never burn bridges**
- Be gracious even if declining
- Industries are small

## Company-Specific Tactics

### For Startups
- Equity is more negotiable than base (preserve cash)
- Ask about acceleration on exit
- Negotiate for advisor equity refresh
- Title often flexible

### For Big Tech
- Bands are real but levels are negotiable
- Focus on level first, comp follows
- Sign-on bridges gaps
- Refreshers matter long-term

### For Late-Stage Private
- RSUs may be coming, understand timing
- Ask about secondary sale opportunities
- Liquidity timeline matters

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do)

- ❌ Fabricate competing offers
- ❌ Threaten or use ultimatums
- ❌ Negotiate publicly (social media, etc.)
- ❌ Accept verbal offers as final
- ❌ Rush the process under artificial pressure
- ❌ Negotiate after accepting
